## QUORA
**Answer this question:** Why are material costs rising faster than labor costs in construction projects?

Material costs are outpacing labor costs in 2025 for a few structural reasons that I'm seeing directly on my jobs as a GC.

First, materials are commodities—they respond to global supply chain dynamics, shipping costs, raw material pricing, and supplier consolidation. Labor, by contrast, is local. A framing crew's rate doesn't jump 11% in two weeks. Material suppliers can and do.

Second, the construction industry has limited leverage on material pricing compared to the past. During the post-pandemic supply crunch, we saw how quickly suppliers could adjust prices when demand exceeded capacity. That volatility hasn't disappeared—it's just more frequent. One call changes your entire job economics.

Third, labor has become more stable because crews are harder to find and retain. Once you have a relationship with a good subcontractor, you protect that relationship. Material suppliers don't have the same incentive structure.

What I'm doing now is building material cost tracking into my project management from day one. I'm not trying to predict the market—I'm monitoring it weekly and building realistic contingencies.
