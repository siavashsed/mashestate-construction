**Answer this question:** What changes did the DOE make to appliance efficiency standards in 2024 that affect construction?

The Department of Energy implemented updated appliance efficiency standards starting in 2024 that directly impact what contractors can legally install in new construction and significant renovation projects. Here's what you need to know from a practical standpoint.

First, this isn't a single rule change—it's a sweeping update affecting multiple appliance categories including HVAC systems, water heaters, refrigeration units, and commercial cooking equipment. Each category has its own timeline and efficiency thresholds. The problem is that the transition happens quietly, and many contractors don't realize their standard equipment specs are no longer compliant until they try to order them.

I discovered this the hard way on a Phoenix multifamily project where three HVAC units I'd been ordering for two years suddenly weren't available through my usual supplier. The manufacturer had discontinued those models to meet new efficiency requirements.

The practical impact: you need to verify every appliance specification against current DOE standards before ordering. This means checking efficiency ratings, ensuring equipment meets minimum SEER, AFUE, or EF ratings depending on the appliance type, and coordinating with suppliers who track these changes.

The biggest challenge is that there's no formal contractor notification system. You either stay proactive about it or find out the hard way mid-project.
